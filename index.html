CFCL continues its creative practice guided by a fundamental question: can fashion contribute to shaping a better society?

As a medium that connects society and the individual wearer, clothing functions as an artwork of multiples, allowing the intentions of its maker to spread through the act of wearing.

With the VOL.12 Collection, CFCL reconceives the concept of “social sculpture” proposed by Joseph Beuys, marking forty years since his passing. By bringing together social activism and art, Beuys sought to create new forms of society and expanded the definition of art itself. Drawing from his practice, which moves across social impact, creative expression and aesthetic awareness, CFCL layers the conceptual notion of sculpture onto Knit-ware.

TWINLAY

The TWINLAY series features a knitted fabric made from a blend of non-mulesed New Zealand wool and Inner Mongolian cashmere. A triple-layered structure that sandwiches highly elastic recycled polyester between layers creates both substantial firmness and warmth. The raw knitted edges and the mélanged surface give the fabric an appearance reminiscent of felt. A generously voluminous unlined coat creates a strong drape at the back by extending a band across the nape of the neck.

AC MILAN / MILAN VELVET

Casual drape is expressed in a contemporary way through the use of multiple lightweight materials. In addition to the signature MILAN series made from 100 percent recycled polyester, the group includes AC MILAN and MILAN VELVET, blended with bright acetate yarn and rayon chenille yarn. Each expresses a delicate drape suited to the Fall/Winter collection, resulting in a light and effortless style.

BARK INSPIRATION

The first motif pattern introduced since the founding of the brand is a foil print inspired by the bark of oak trees. It serves as an homage to Joseph Beuys and his project 7000 Oaks, realized in Kassel, Germany.

SOFT PORTRAIT FOIL / SOFT MILAN FOIL

The tight-fitting SOFT PORTRAIT FOIL dress is knitted in a seamless rib structure using cupro polyester yarn, then finished with a foil print applied to the completed garment. The SOFT MILAN FOIL flared dress combines an upper body knitted in seamless rib with metallic yarn and a fan-shaped skirt printed with foil.

FLUFFY FRINGE FOIL / ENCHANT

The oak bark motif is rendered in three-dimensional texture and developed into special occasion dresses. The FLUFFY FRINGE FOIL dress features plate-like knitted fringe finished with foil print together with metallic thread fringe. The ENCHANT train dress is further elevated with sequins, enhancing its elegance.

The collaboration with VEJA began from a shared commitment to greater transparency within the sneaker supply chain. In this second edition, CFCL continues its exploration of functional design and innovative materials.

Incorporating CFCL’s sophisticated style and proposing knitwear as tools for people to use in their everyday lives, this exclusive edition reflects a reductive design approach and meticulous attention to quality materials.

The collaboration is scheduled for release in early fall 2026.

Ben Vida

Ben Vida is a composer, writer and artist. His practice encompasses works for voice and ensemble, Musique Concrète and electro-acoustic improvisation. He has presented pieces at Présences Electronique, Unsound Festival, ReWire Festival, eFlux, MoMA, DIA:Beacon, ZKM, BAM and the Semibreve Festival.

Recent releases include a collaboration with the Yarn/Wire Ensemble titled “The Beat My Head Hit” and “Vocal Trio”. His new record Oblivion Seekers will be released in Spring 2026 on Shelter Press.

About CFCL

CFCL stands for “Clothing For Contemporary Life”. “Simplicity”, “Modesty”, “Responsibility” are core values the brand heralds.

CFCL designs clothing for people following a timeless approach and ethos, using materials which contribute sustainability and state-of-the-art technology.
